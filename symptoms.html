Symptoms of HIV

HIV symptoms vary depending on the stage of infection. In the early stages, symptoms may be mild or absent, but as the disease progresses, more noticeable symptoms appear. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment.

Common Symptoms:

Fever

One of the most common early symptoms of HIV infection, often appearing within the first few weeks.

Fatigue

Chronic tiredness and exhaustion, even after adequate rest, is a frequent symptom of HIV.

Unexplained Weight Loss

Sudden and significant weight loss can indicate HIV progression towards AIDS.

Skin Rashes

Various skin conditions and rashes may appear, especially during the acute phase of HIV.
